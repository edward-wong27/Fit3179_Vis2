Victorian Crash Statistics 2000 - 2005

Car Crashes Around Victoria

This map highlights all the locations that crashes have occured around victoria from 2000 - 2005.

As shown in the map the density of these crashes

is higher towords the inner cities and suburs around

Melbourne's CBD where a higher percentage of the population resides.

To interact with this map select the region you are interested in looking at

Then zoom in with the provided zoom slider

Crashes by Region

Is there specific regions that are at a higher risk of crashes?

The line chart on the left follows the amount of

chrashes that occur each month from 2000 - 2005, categorised

by the region that the crash occured in.

The density of crashes in regions around Melbourne's CBD is

quantified in this graph as the "METROPOLITAN NORTH WEST REGION"

and "METROPOLITAN SOUTH EAST REGION"see considerably higher amounts of

crashes per month compared to other regions.

Total Crashes per Month

Is there a vehicle type that is more crash prone

compared to other modes of transport?

The bar chart on the left is the summation of

crashes that occur each month from 2000 – 2005 and is

broken down into different vehicle types. Popular and common vehicle

types such as passenger cars and trucks appear in significantly

more crashes as compared to other modes of transport, this

and trucks on the road

Injury Level by Age

Is there a certain age group that is at a higher risk of injury?

The heat map on the left aims to identify such

risk groups by seeing which groups see the most amounts

of injuries. Injury levels range from 1-4, where;

4 – No Injury

3 – Other Injury

2 – Serious Injury

1 – Fatality

We can observe that the age groups between 17-59 see

more crashes and see higher numbers of fatalities.

The 30-39 age group experience the most crashes however majority

of them are either not injured or have other injuries.
